*Spectroscopy* is a powerful scientific technique used to study the interaction between matter and electromagnetic radiation. In the realm of molecular spectroscopy, it specifically focuses on analyzing the unique ways molecules interact with light, allowing scientists to deduce valuable information about their structure, composition, and behavior.

This branch of science encompasses various spectroscopic methods such as infrared (IR), ultraviolet-visible (UV-Vis), nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR), and Raman spectroscopy, among others. Each method offers distinct insights into molecular properties by probing different regions of the electromagnetic spectrum.
